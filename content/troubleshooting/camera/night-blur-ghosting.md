title: Night Blur/Ghosting
summary: Troubleshooting steps for blurry or ghosting images at night or in low-light scenes.
order: 40
keywords: [camera, night, ghosting, low light, wdr, dnr, shutter, exposure]
---

1. For general low-light ghosting, set Supplement Light Brightness Control to manual and adjust brightness to 100. Improve the ambient lighting conditions where possible.

![Supplement light brightness setting](assets/images/camera/camera-doc-image-01.png)

2. If ghosting is caused by slow shutter speed, increase the shutter speed or reduce the exposure time, for example to 1/50. Note that too short an exposure time may darken the image.

![Exposure time setting](assets/images/camera/camera-doc-image-02.png)

3. If ghosting is caused by temporal noise reduction, set Digital Noise Reduction to Expert Mode and reduce Time DNR Level to 30-40. Image noise may increase; you can raise Space DNR Level to 60-70 as needed.

![Digital noise reduction settings](assets/images/camera/camera-doc-image-03.png)

4. Reduce sharpness to around 30 and increase contrast to 60-70 to reduce noise interference.

![Sharpness and contrast settings](assets/images/camera/camera-doc-image-04.png)

5. If WDR is enabled in a low-light scene and causes ghosting, disable WDR or set WDR to Auto. WDR is intended for strong-light or backlight scenes.
